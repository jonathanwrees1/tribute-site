Louis Pasteur

~The Father of Microbiology~

Timeline of Louis Pasteur's Life

  • 1822-Born in Dole, Jura, France
  • 1831-Pasteur entered primary school
  • 1839-He entered the Collège Royal at Besançon to study philosophy
  • 1846-Appointed professor of physics at the Collège de Tournon
  • 1848-Became professor of chemistry at the University of Strasbourg
  • 1849-Married Marie Laurent
  • 1854-Named dean of the new faculty of sciences at University of Lille, where he began his studies on fermentation.
  • 1857 -Pasteur sent a paper about lactic acid fermentation to the Société des Sciences de Lille
  • 1861-He observed that less sugar fermented per part of yeast when the yeast was exposed to air. The lower rate of fermentation aerobically became known as the Pasteur effect.
  • 1865-Pasteur's research also showed that the growth of micro-organisms was responsible for spoiling beverages, such as beer, wine and milk. He invented a process in which liquids such as milk were heated to a temperature between 60 and 100 °C. This killed most bacteria and moulds already present within them. The method became known as pasteurization
  • 1885-Invented the rabies vaccine which proved to be highly effective
  • 1895-Died at the age of 72 most likely due to a stroke.

Louis Pasteur's research in chemistry led to remarkable breakthroughs in the understanding of the causes and preventions of diseases, which laid down the foundations of hygiene, public health and much of modern medicine. His works are credited to saving millions of lives through the developments of vaccines.
